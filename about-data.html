About Property Value Unlock

What This Site Does

This site helps homeowners determine whether their property can be split, subdivided, partially sold, or valued differently based on what the land can actually support, and what that may mean financially.

Property Value Unlock provides factual, practical information for homeowners who want to understand whether they can split, subdivide, or sell part of their residential property. The site explains how lot splitting works, what the municipal approval process involves, and how to evaluate the development potential of land.

Who This Is For

This site is for homeowners in suburban Illinois who own larger residential lots and want to understand their options. This includes people who:

  • Own a home on two or more lots and want to sell one
  • Have a large backyard and want to know if it can be split off and sold
  • Are considering whether to subdivide before selling their full property
  • Want to understand what their extra land is worth as a buildable lot
  • Need to know about frontage, utility access, and zoning requirements before proceeding

Problems This Site Solves

Homeowners often do not know whether their lot qualifies for a split, what steps are involved, or what the land could be worth. This site addresses that gap by covering:

  • How to determine if a lot split is legally and physically possible
  • What zoning and bulk requirements typically govern lot splits in Illinois suburbs
  • How frontage and lot width affect whether a new lot can be created
  • Whether utilities such as water, sewer, and electricity can serve a newly created lot
  • How to estimate land value based on development potential rather than assessed value
  • When it makes more financial sense to split before selling versus selling the full property

Topics Covered

  • Lot splitting and subdivision in Illinois
  • Selling part of a yard or backyard parcel
  • What happens when a house sits on two separate lots
  • Frontage requirements and minimum lot width
  • Utility availability and access for new lots
  • How land value is determined by development potential
  • Comparing a lot split strategy against selling the whole property

Geographic Focus

This site focuses on residential property owners in the following Illinois counties:

  • McHenry County, IL
  • Kane County, IL
  • DuPage County, IL
  • Lake County, IL
  • Suburban Cook County, IL

Information reflects conditions, zoning practices, and municipal processes typical to these suburban Chicago-area counties. Specific rules vary by municipality.

Content Structure

Each page on this site addresses a specific question a homeowner might have about splitting or selling part of their property. Pages are written to be direct and informative, without requiring prior knowledge of real estate or zoning law.
